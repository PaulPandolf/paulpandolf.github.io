CHANGE OF COMMAND

NARRATOR:

“KEY TO THE CHANGE OF COMMAND IS THE PASSING OF THE UNIT’S COLORS. THESE COLORS REPRESENT NOT ONLY THE HERITAGE AND HISTORY OF THE UNIT, BUT ALSO THE UNITY AND LOYALTY OF ITS SOLDIERS. SINCE THE EARLY DAYS OF WARFARE, COLORS HAVE SERVED AS THE TALISMAN OF UNIT IDENTITY. THE FLAG WAS SYMBOLIC. IT HELPED UNITS DEVELOP A SENSE OF PRIDE AND ESPRIT DE CORPS, AS WELL AS SERVING THE MORE PRACTICAL PURPOSE OF MARKING THE LOCATION OF THE COMMANDER, AND PROVIDING A RALLY POINT FOR SOLDIERS DURING THE SMOKE AND CHAOS OF BATTLE. THE COLORS ARE THE COMMANDER’S SYMBOL OF AUTHORITY, REPRESENTING HIS RESPONSIBILITIES TO THE ORGANIZATION. WHEREVER THE COMMANDER IS, SO ARE THE COLORS. THE CUSTODIAN OF THE COLORS IS THE COMMAND SERGEANT MAJOR, AS THE SENIOR ENLISTED SOLDIER IN THE UNIT, AND PRINCIPLE ADVISOR TO THE COMMANDER.”

NARRATOR:

“THE PASSING OF THE COLORS SYMBOLIZES THE TRANSFER OF COMMAND RESPONSIBILITY AND AUTHORITY. THE CEREMONY BEGINS AS COMMAND SERGEANT MAJOR CURRENT CSM LAST NAME, PASSES THE COLORS TO OUTGOING COMMANDER RANK, LAST FOR THE LAST TIME. OUTGOING COMMANDER RANK, LAST WILL THEN PASS THE COLORS TO REVIEWING OFFICER RANK, LAST, THEREBY RELINQUISHING HIS RESPONSIBILITY AND AUTHORITY. REVIEWING OFFICER RANK, LAST WILL THEN PASS THE COLORS TO INCOMING COMMANDER RANK, LAST, CHARGING THE NEW COMMANDER WITH THE SAME RESPONSIBILITIES AND AUTHORITY.”

CUE: Passing the colors starts on effective.

“EFFECTIVE CEREMONY DATE, OUTGOING COMMANDER RANK, FIRST MI. LAST RELINQUISHES COMMAND OF THE UNIT.”

CUE: Narrator will wait to read until the senior commander has the color. By authority cue passing the colors to the incoming commander.

“BY AUTHORITY OF PARAGRAPH 2-5, ARMY REGULATION 600–20, THE UNDERSIGNED ASSUMES COMMAND OF THE UNIT, SIGNED, INCOMING COMMANDER RANK, FIRST MI. LAST, COMMANDING.”

CUE: The incoming commander passes the colors back to the CSM after commanding.
